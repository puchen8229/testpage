Price Control and Quantity Control Welfare Lab

This standalone simulator now has two layers. In Explore and measure, the user can study price ceilings, price floors, and quantity caps / quotas, reset all the way back to pure market equilibrium, and drag the missing market outcome line: the realized quantity under price controls and the realized price under quantity controls. In Practice and check, the user must place the control line and the realized market line, then fill in how consumer surplus, producer surplus, economic surplus, and deadweight loss change against the initial equilibrium.

Assumptions used for the shaded areas
  • Demand and supply are linear, and the original market starts at the competitive equilibrium.
  • The Reset to pure market equilibrium button removes every control and restores the original equilibrium outcome.
  • Under a binding price ceiling or binding price floor, the legal price is controlled and realized trade can be dragged up to the short side of the market.
  • Under a binding quota, the legal quantity is fixed first and the realized transaction price can be dragged anywhere inside the feasible band between supply and demand at that quantity.
  • Economic surplus here means consumer surplus + producer surplus. No separate government purchase program or tax revenue is included in this price-control / quota tool.

In practice mode, the user must place the control line and the realized outcome line first. Then choose how each welfare measure changes relative to the initial equilibrium. For binding price controls, the canonical answer uses the short-side quantity. For binding quotas, the canonical practice answer uses the midpoint price inside the feasible band so there is one definite answer to check.
