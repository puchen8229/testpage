Elasticity and incidence reminder
At the initial equilibrium, approximate elasticities are computed from the slope sliders:
|Ed| ≈ P* / (Q* × demand slope)
Es ≈ P* / (Q* × supply slope)

The standard incidence rule then appears clearly:
buyers' share = Es / (Es + |Ed|)
sellers' share = |Ed| / (Es + |Ed|)

Because the curves rotate around the same no-tax equilibrium, changes in incidence here come only from changing the slopes and elasticities. At the top slider value, the curve switches to the perfectly inelastic vertical-limit case, so the graph focuses on incidence, revenue, quantity, and deadweight loss.
